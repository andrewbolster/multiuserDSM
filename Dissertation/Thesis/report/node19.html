DSM Level 3

Level 3 techniques rely on the concept of signal vectoring, coordinated at a DSLAM, such that all lines are terminated on common equipment. Complete signal vectoring eliminates cross-talk through the generation of a cancellation matrix consisting of block-permuted, diagonal Inverse Discrete Fourier Transform matrices, acting as a Generalised Decision Feedback Equaliser, which, as demonstrated in Cioffi (1997), can eliminate cross-talk, as well ISI in the case of impulse envelope skewing over long lines.

Vectored-DMT (VDMTVDMTVectored DMT) can produce optimal bundle efficiencies, but the conditions on VDMT's operation2.15 make it inapplicable for commercial use.

Fortunately, DSL Level 3 is not the subject of this project.
