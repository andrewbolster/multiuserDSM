% ABSTRACT
\chapter*{\centering \begin{normalsize}Executive Summary\end{normalsize}}
\begin{quotation}
\noindent The security of information transmitted over an 802.11 wireless link is of paramount importance in a world where both wireless networks and the sharing of personal data online are increasing at rapid pace. However with the increase in popularity of wireless networks comes an increased risk of network attack. Detecting these attacks is a difficult task and currently requires a combination of additional hardware and networking knowledge. This thesis investigates the viability of an access-point based distributed intrusion detection system, which uses an existing wireless devices to monitor the WLAN, report statistics to an analysis server, and manage the WLANs clients. Ultimately this approach proved successful in terms of both the cost of implementation and detection ability.
\end{quotation}
